Boothstown Village Hall Association Constitution
1)NAME

The name of the group shall be the Boothstown Village Hall Association, hereafter referred to as the Association. The abbreviation BVH may also be used.


2)AIMS

The aims of the Association shall be:

a) to manage the building with a lease from the Manchester Diocese

b) to promote the wellbeing of the inhabitants of Boothstown without distinction of sex, age or of political or religious beliefs, by associating the Local Authority, voluntary organisations and inhabitants in a common effort to advance education and to provide facilities in the interest of social welfare for recreation, physical and mental training and social, moral and intellectual development.

c) to further the improvement of the quality of life for the residents of Boothstown and surrounding districts


3)POWERS

In furtherance of the aims, but not otherwise, the membership will elect a Management Committee who will have overall responsibility for the management of the Association and for the day to day management and hire of the Boothstown Village Hall.

The Management Committee will have the following powers:

a) power to raise funds and to invite and receive contributions, provided that in raising funds the committee shall not undertake any substantial trading activities and shall conform to any relevant requirements of the law.

b) power to do such other lawful things as are necessary for the achievement of the aims.


4)MEMBERSHIP

a) Membership of the Association will be open to anyone who has an interest in helping the Association to achieve its aims, is willing to abide by the rules of the Association and willing to pay any subscription agreed by the Management Committee . A list of members will be kept by the Secretary.

b) Every member, after three months of continuous membership, will be deemed to be a full member of the association and shall have one vote at General Meetings.

c) The membership of any member may be refused or terminated for good reason by the Management Committee.


5)MANAGEMENT

a) The Association shall be administered by a Management Committee of not less than 4 and not more than 8 members elected at the Annual General Meeting.

b) The Management Committee shall meet at least 6 times a year.

c) The chairperson shall chair all meetings of the Association. If the chair is absent from any meeting, the vice-chairperson shall chair the meeting. In the absence of both the chairperson and the vice-chairperson the members of the committee present will choose one of their number to chair the meeting before any business is transacted.

d) The officers of the Management Committee shall be the Chairperson, the Vice-chairperson, the Treasurer and the Secretary.

e) The quorum for the Management Committee meetings shall be three.

f) Voting at Management Committee meetings shall be by a show of hands. If there is a tied vote then the Chairperson shall have a second vote. Any person with a vested interest in an item on the agenda must declare that interest and will not be allowed a vote on that item.


6)THE DUTIES OF THE OFFICERS

a) The duties of the Chairperson shall be:

1) to preside at meetings of the Committee and the Association

2) as may be required by the Committee to act as the principal spokesperson on public occasions or when representations are being made on behalf of the Association to public bodies.

b) The duties of the Vice- chairperson shall be:

1) to deputise in the absence of the Chairperson

c) The duties of the Secretary shall be:

1) to prepare, in consultation with the Chairperson, the agenda for the meetings of the Committee

2) to take the minutes of all meetings

d) The duties of the Treasurer shall be:

1) to supervise the financial affairs of the Association

2) to ensure that proper accounts are kept with regards to all monies received and paid out by the Association. The accounts will be examined annually by an independent examiner who is not a member of the Association.

e) The Boothstown Village Hall Association is a Company limited by guarantee. Management Committee members should be prepared but are not obliged to be directors of the Company.


7)FINANCE

a) Any money obtained by the Association shall only be used to further the Association’s aims

b) Any bank accounts opened for the Association shall be in the name of the Association.

c) Any cheques issued shall be signed by two of the three committee members nominated as signatories. Cheques may not be signed on behalf of the Association by two related persons.


8)ANNUAL GENERAL MEETING

a) The Association shall hold an Annual General Meeting (AGM) in the month of October

b) All members shall be given at least 14 days notice of the AGM and shall be entitled to attend and vote.

c) The business of the AGM shall include:

1) receiving a report from the Chairperson on the Association’s activities over the year

2) receiving a report from the Treasurer on the finances of the Association

3) electing a new Management Committee

4) appointing an independent examiner of the accounts

5) and considering any other matter as may be decided

d) The quorum for the AGM and any General meeting shall be 20 persons

e) Nominations and resolutions should be received in writing by the Secretary seven days prior to the date of the AGM.


9)SPECIAL GENERAL MEETING

A Special General Meeting may be called by the Management Committee or members to discuss an urgent matter. The Secretary shall give all members fourteen days notice of any Special General Meeting together with notice of the business to be discussed.


10)ALTERATIONS TO THE CONSTITUTION

Any changes to this Constitution must be agreed by at least two-thirds of those members present and voting at any General Meeting.


11)DISSOLUTION

The Association may be wound up at any time if agreed by two-thirds of those present and voting at any General Meeting. In the event of winding up any assets remaining after all debts have been paid shall be given to another group with similar charitable aims.

About us

Boothstown Village Hall is a historic building in the village of Boothstown, Salford.
The hall was a school building from 1874 until 2011, when the school decided that the building was no longer required.
In order to preserve the building for future generations, local residents formed the Boothstown Village Hall Association, a charity that now maintains and runs it as a village hall for the local community.
The village hall can be hired for activities and community events, for details of upcoming events see the Events page.
